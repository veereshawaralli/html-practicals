Vegetable Biryani

A simple veg biryani recipe using only HTML lists for ingredients and steps.

Details

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 35-45 minutes
  • Serves: 4

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrot, peas, beans, potato)
  • 2 onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 2 tbsp biryani masala
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder (optional)
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • Handful fresh mint and coriander leaves
  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
  • Whole spices: 1 bay leaf, 2 cloves, 2 cardamom, 1 small cinnamon stick
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed

Equipment

  • Large pot for rice
  • Deep pan or Dutch oven for layering and dum
  • Spatula and ladle

Preparation Steps

  1. Rinse basmati rice under cold water until water runs clear. Soak for 20 minutes, then drain.
  2. Chop vegetables, slice onions, and chop tomatoes. Keep yogurt ready.
  3. Measure spices and prepare whole spices.

Cooking Steps

  1. Boil water in a large pot with a little salt and whole spices. Add soaked rice and cook until 70% done. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat oil/ghee in a deep pan. Add whole spices and fry for a few seconds until aromatic.
  3. Add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
  4. Stir in ginger-garlic paste and green chilies; cook for 1 minute. Add tomatoes and cook until soft.
  5. Add turmeric, red chili powder, biryani masala, and salt. Mix well.
  6. Add mixed vegetables and yogurt. Cook on medium heat until vegetables are partly tender.
  7. Layer half of the par-cooked rice over the vegetable mixture. Sprinkle some mint, coriander and a little garam masala.
  8. Add the remaining rice in a final layer. Top with remaining mint, coriander and fried onions if available.
  9. Cover the pan with a tight lid and cook on low heat (dum) for 15–20 minutes to let flavors meld.
  10. Turn off heat, let rest for 5 minutes, then gently fluff the biryani with a fork and serve hot.

Serving Suggestions

  • Serve with raita (yogurt with cucumber and spices) or a side salad.
  • Garnish with lemon wedges and extra fresh herbs.
